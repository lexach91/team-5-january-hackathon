Sleep

"A poor night’s sleep is likely to leave us feeling irritable, tired and lethargic and can affect how we perform at work or home the next day. If your sleep is disrupted for a number of nights, you’re likely to find it difficult to concentrate and make sound decisions."

Useless knowledge

Useless knowledge could be useful... and sometimes it could put you to sleep. Here are some random facts for you:

Improve your sleep

Get into a routine.

Make sure you go to sleep around the same time each night this will help your body’s internal clock and leave you feeling more refreshed each morning.

Have some 'you time'.

Try to relax before getting some sleep for example:

  • Read a book
  • Have a bath
  • Practice Mindfullness

Keep a sleep diary.

Sleep diaries allow you to spot patterns that may be causing restless nights such as diet, stress, or caffeine. This is also helpful if you decide to see a GP or sleep specialist.

Sleep Article One

Get a better nights sleep.

Recovering from a bad night's sleep.

Take a cold shower.

Taking a cold shower increases your heart rate, blood flow and oxygen intake boosting your overall energy leaving you feeling energised and more awake.

Soak up some sun.

Exposure to sun can help to wake you up by boosting your levels of serotonin. This will improve your mood and increase your energy levels

Listen to some music.

Music can help to raise your attention levels leaving you more alert whilst also boosting your mood and overall wellbeing. Just make sure you choose something upbeat!

Sleep Article Two

Recovering from a bad nights sleep.

Get better at power napping.

Duration is Key.

The key to a successful powernap is its length. Try to keep your power naps between 15-20 minutes this will leave you energised and more awake.

Remove Distractions

Eliminate distractions to ensure your nap is undisturbed. This includes closing laptop screens, diverting calls and turning off bright lights.
Happy Napping!

Find your nappy place.

Choose a spot which is likely to be quiet, undisturbed and cozy. To ensure you wake in a good mood make sure your spot is clean and fresh.

Sleep Article Three

Get better at power napping.

Interpret your dreams.

Record your Dreams.

This is the first step to interpreting the meaning of your dreams isto record them. As soon as you wake write a few sentences to encapsulate the dream taking unconscious thoughts into reality.

Identify your emotions

Consider your emotions within the dream and whether you seemed comfortable. According to reasearch we are often called by unconscious thoughts to reflect on emotions in daily life.

Look at the big picture.

Don't over analyse your dreams and try to avoid bring negative emotions into reality. Accept that parts of your dreams could just be irrational thoughts.
