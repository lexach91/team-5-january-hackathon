Exercise

"Physical activity has been shown to have a positive impact on our mood. A study asked people to rate their mood immediately after periods of physical activity (e.g. going for a walk or doing housework), and periods of inactivity (e.g. reading a book or watching television). "

Stay active

It does not matter what you do, but try to stay active. Here are some ideas for you:

Punch through your worries

Physical/ Mental Strength.

In addition to physical strength boxing can help you to feel more powerful mentally whilst improving confidence and self-esteem.

Overcome Anger

Physically hitting a punching bag produces a response in your body that reduces tension and stress. Boxing is a safe way to release anger that has built up in your body.

Switch Off

Boxing requires you to fully concentrate on the task at hand. To box well you must switch off from to outside world and be 100% focused.

Relieve anxiety through swimming

Blue in Colour.

Research shows that being around predominently blue coloured places improved mental wellbeing. Pale blue tones calm the mind and aid concentration.

Social Interaction.

Although swimming is a solitary form of physical exercise it's also a hobby and team sport that can be shared with others. Swimming is a great way to meet like minded people.

Healthy Brain.

Being immersed in water boosts blood flow to the brain which increases oxygen to the brain having a positive impact on brain health.

Go Outdoors

New Activities

Taking part in a new outdoor activity comes with many health benefits with the added bonus of fresh air! Your body will release endorphins leaving you feeling happier.

Natural Beauty.

Being outdoors allows you to to appreciate the beautiful world that we live in. You will be able to watch the world go by, leaving your headspace peaceful and rejuventated.

Vitamin D

Research shows that vitamin D helps to regulate your mood and combat depression. Enjoying the sun is an amazing benfit of being outdoors.

Exercise Article Two

Go Outdoors.

Get your steps in

Take the stairs.

Climbing stairs is a brilliant way to get the heart pumping and can be done in the comfort of your own home. Set yourself a target each day and try to reach it!

Treadmill Desks.

Check with your company and see if they provide treadmill desks. Walking whilst typing takes some getting used to but its a brilliant way to keep your brain active.

Park far away.

Try to avoid taking the easy route to places. Park further away from the shops to help you get in your steps and give you more time outdoors.
